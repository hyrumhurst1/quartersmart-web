Case Study · Structure Properties

Structure Properties: from handing out SOPs to a full AI training system

The problem

At Structure Properties, training used to run on memory and paperwork. When an employee made a mistake, they were handed the SOP and told to rewrite it by hand. Like a middle-school punishment. The thinking was that copying the document out longhand would make the lesson stick.

It did not scale. New hires slogged through dense documents. The same questions came back week after week. Owners had no real way to see who knew what, or where people kept getting stuck. The knowledge lived in binders and in a few people's heads.

"Our SOPs were something you got punished with, not something you learned from. Now the whole company runs on them."

What we built

QuarterSmart built the AI training and onboarding layer that Structure Properties runs on today.

  • A 39-course video library spanning seven operational roles, with AI-automated course and video creation so the library can grow without a production team.
  • Claude-powered quizzes that check real understanding, not just attendance.
  • Automated certification and compliance reporting baked in.
  • An owner dashboard that shows who has completed what, and exactly where people struggle on quizzes.

The same SOPs that used to be a punishment now do double duty. Each one became a training course the team learns from and the backbone of how the work gets verified. Employees can review any process in a click instead of hunting through a document. The system is deployed on Vercel.

The result

  • Onboarding time and repeat questions cut by 65 percent.
  • SOP-related mistakes cut by 36 percent.
  • Coverage across seven operational roles, with automated certification and compliance reporting.

The lesson still sticks. Nobody has to copy a document by hand to make it happen.

How we measured it

We baseline before we build. For Structure Properties that meant tracking onboarding time, repeat questions, and SOP-related mistakes across seven roles, then comparing against the same measures after the system went live. We agree on the metrics that matter to you up front so the result is something you can verify, not a claim you have to take on faith.
