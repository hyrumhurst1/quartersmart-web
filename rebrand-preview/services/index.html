What we do: train your team, automate the rest

Your SOPs already do one job. We make them do two.

Every team has standard operating procedures. Most of them sit in a folder nobody opens. We take that same source material and turn it into two working things at once.

First, a training course your team learns from. AI-generated video, quizzes, and certification, so a new hire can watch how the job is done and prove they understood it.

Second, an automation that runs the task itself. Built in n8n, Zapier, or Power Automate, so the repetitive part of the work happens without anyone touching it.

Same source. Two outputs. One SOP becomes both how your people learn the job and how the work gets done without them. Write it once, and it teaches and it runs.

That is the mechanic underneath everything below. Here is how an engagement actually moves.

1. AI Enablement and Training

This is the door. Before we automate anything, we get your team comfortable using AI in their day-to-day work.

We train your people on the tools that fit how they already operate. Real prompts, real workflows, real tasks from your business, not a generic seminar. The goal is simple. Your team stops being scared of AI and starts using it on Monday.

For a lot of clients, this is enough to start. It also tells us exactly where the manual pain lives, which is what we automate next.

2. SOPs to Training System

Next, we turn your procedures into a real training and onboarding system.

We take your SOPs, the messy ones and the good ones, and convert each into an AI-generated course. Video walkthroughs that show the work. Quizzes that confirm understanding. Certification that proves someone is cleared to do the job.

Owners and managers get a reporting dashboard. You see who has completed what, who is certified, and where people consistently struggle on quizzes. When everyone misses the same question, that is not a people problem. That is an SOP problem, and now you can see it.

The AI-generated video is the part clients underestimate. New hires do not read a fourteen-page document. They watch the process, click through the steps, and review any task again in one click instead of slogging through a file. Onboarding stops being a stack of paperwork and becomes something people actually finish.

3. SOPs to Automations

Now we take the long, painful, manual tasks and build them into automations.

The same procedures we turned into training also describe work that machines should be doing. Lead capture and follow-up. Appointment scheduling and reminders. Client onboarding steps. Reporting that someone copies and pastes every Friday. We build those into automations using n8n, Zapier, or Power Automate, and we bundle them into the engagement.

This is where the double duty pays off. Your people are trained on the process, and the most repetitive parts of that process now run on their own. The team handles judgment. The system handles the grind.

We have published 25 automation templates to the n8n public library with more than 10,000 total uses, covering AI video production, home-services lead capture and SMS scheduling, client onboarding, and property-management operations. We build from a real track record, not a slide deck.

4. Coaching and Consulting Calls

Software does not stick on its own. People have questions, and questions do not wait for the next quarter.

We run recurring coaching and consulting calls, bi-weekly or monthly, where anyone on your team can hop on. Stuck on a workflow, want to build something new, need a second set of eyes on a process, bring it to the call. It keeps the system in use instead of slowly going stale.

When you need more than a coaching call, when it is a real "help me build this" project, we take it on as additional consulting. You get a builder on demand without hiring one full time.

5. Hosting and Maintenance: the Care Plan

Every engagement ends at a recurring baseline, not at zero.

We host and maintain your training system and your automations on a monthly care plan. We keep the integrations running, update courses as your processes change, and fix things before they become your problem. Tools break, APIs change, and procedures evolve. The care plan is what keeps the whole thing alive after launch.

This is how a project becomes a relationship. You are not left with a system you have to babysit. You have a partner who keeps it working.
