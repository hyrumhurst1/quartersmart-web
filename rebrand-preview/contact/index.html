What happens next

No long discovery deck. No pressure. Here is the actual path.

  1. A short call. Fifteen to thirty minutes. You tell us how your team onboards, where the repeat questions pile up, and which tasks eat the most hours.
  2. We map your SOPs. We look at what you already document, what lives in people's heads, and what gets explained the same way every week.
  3. We find the first build. We point at the one place where turning an SOP into a course plus an automation pays off fastest. That becomes your starting point, not a six-month roadmap.
  4. You get a proposal. Clear scope, clear outcomes, no surprises. You decide from there.

That is it. The goal of the first call is to find the highest-value first build, not to sell you everything at once.

Frequently asked questions

What happens after I book a call?
We do a short call to learn how your team onboards and where the repeat work lives, then map your SOPs and point at the single highest-value first build. After that you get a proposal with clear scope and outcomes. No pressure, no long discovery process.
Do I need my SOPs written down before we talk?
No. Most of what your team does well lives in people's heads, and part of our job is pulling it out. Bring one process that frustrates your team and we will tell you on the call whether it is a strong first candidate.
How much does it cost?
It depends on the roles, SOPs, and automations in scope, so we put pricing in your proposal rather than on the site. Book a call or request a proposal and we will scope it to what you actually need.
